Bullying

                   Bullying is a harmful behavior characterized by intentional, repetitive actions aimed at hurting or controlling someone physically, emotionally, or socially. It can take various forms, such as physical bullying (hitting, pushing), verbal bullying (insults, name-calling), social bullying (spreading rumors, exclusion), and cyberbullying (harassment through digital platforms). Victims of bullying may show signs like unexplained injuries, avoidance of social situations, changes in behavior, or a decline in academic performance. The effects of bullying can be severe, including anxiety, depression, low self-esteem, physical health issues, and long-term difficulties in relationships or mental health.
